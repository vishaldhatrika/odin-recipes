Rajma Chawal

Rajma Chawal is a classic North Indian dish that exemplifies comfort food at its finest. This hearty and wholesome meal consists of red kidney beans (rajma) cooked in a flavorful tomato-based gravy, seasoned with a medley of spices such as cumin, coriander, garam masala, and chili powder.

The dish is typically served alongside steamed basmati rice (chawal), creating a fulfilling and nutritious combination. Rajma Chawal is not only delicious but also a staple in many Indian households, cherished for its simplicity and satisfying flavors. It's a dish that brings people together, evoking memories of home-cooked meals and warm gatherings with loved ones.

Ingredients

  • Red kidney beans (rajma)
  • Basmati rice
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Garam masala
  • Chili powder
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Soak the red kidney beans (rajma) overnight in water.
  2. Drain the soaked rajma and rinse it with fresh water.
  3. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked rajma, water, and salt. Cook for about 15-20 minutes or until the rajma is tender.
  4. In a separate pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once the cumin seeds splutter, add chopped onions, ginger, and garlic. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, coriander powder, garam masala, and chili powder. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the spices are well combined.
  6. Add the cooked rajma to the tomato mixture and mix well. Simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  7. In a separate pot, cook basmati rice according to package instructions.
  8. Serve the rajma curry alongside steamed basmati rice. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and enjoy!
